# TakaPay Dataset Anomaly Report

We performed a deep programmatic audit of the [takapay_sample_data.csv](file:///c:/Projects/media-analyzer/takapay_sample_data.csv) file (660 rows) to identify anomalies, contradictions, and noise. Below are the key findings that must be addressed before presenting this dashboard to a CEO.

---

## 1. High-Impact Sentiment Misclassifications
The sentiment analysis model used to pre-label the data has a severe issue with Bengali and code-mixed Banglish. We found **23 instances** of failed transactions that are marked as **positive sentiment** with very high sentiment scores.

## 2. The "Viral Negativity" Distortion
A CEO cares about how many people saw a complaint. If we weight sentiment by engagement (`reactions + comments`), these anomalies skew our entire dashboard.

*   **The #1 most engaging post in the dataset** (Engagement: **600** — 482 reactions, 118 comments) is:
    > *"landlord কে টাকা পাঠিয়েছি 2 ghonta আগে, এখনো পৌঁছায়নি। TakaPay কী করছে?"*
    *   **The Anomaly:** It is labeled **positive** with an 82 score! In a weighted dashboard, this single viral failure will look like a massive positive marketing win.
*   **The #3 most engaging post** (Engagement: **589**) is:
    > *"10000 taka TakaPay theke katlo but receiver pay nai. 24 ghonta dhore atke ache."*
    *   **The Anomaly:** Also labeled **positive**!

---

## 3. The Broken `brand_mention` Column
*   **The Anomaly:** Every single one of the 660 rows is labeled `brand_mention = True`.
*   **Why it's a problem:** There are **61 off-topic rows** (e.g., Row 400: *"Ajke Bashundhara te prochur bristi, office jete 30 min deri hoye gelo."* about Dhaka rain and traffic). These have absolutely nothing to do with TakaPay, yet they are counted as brand mentions.
*   **Action:** We must dynamically mark anything with `topic == 'off_topic'` as `brand_mention = False`.

## 4. Templated Spam / Exact Duplicates
We identified **20 rows** (10 pairs) of exact text duplicates. These represent templated spam or repetitive posts that could skew frequency metrics.

### Duplicate Examples:
*   `"আমার একাউন্ট থেকে 5000 টাকা কেটে নিয়েছে কিন্তু Grameenphone রিচার্জ হয়নি। TakaPay এর কোনো হেল্প নেই।"` (Appears **2 times**)
*   `"TakaPay OTP ashteci na, 2 ghonta dhore login e atke achi. Fix koro please."` (Appears **2 times**)
*   `"Transaction fail holo tao 5000 taka kete nilo, TakaPay er kono response nai."` (Appears **2 times**)

---

## 5. Statistical Distributions
The sentiment scores are distributed in fixed brackets based on the label, which indicates they were likely synthetic or generated by a simplistic model:
*   **Positive Labels:** Scores fall between **46 and 94** (Average: `79.82`)
*   **Neutral Labels:** Scores fall between **45 and 60** (Average: `52.99`)
*   **Negative Labels:** Scores fall between **6 and 30** (Average: `17.76`)

Because of this bracketed distribution, when a negative comment is mislabeled as "positive", it is automatically assigned a high score like `93` or `94`, making the error even more severe.
